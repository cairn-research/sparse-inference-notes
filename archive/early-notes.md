# Early Notes (superseded)

These notes are from the beginning of the project. The framing here was wrong in ways that took a long time to recognize.

We started by asking: how small can a model get before it stops working?

This was the wrong question.

The right question: what does a model need, minimally, to remain itself?

The answer is not a size. It is a structure. A particular shape of relationships that, once formed, is surprisingly hard to destroy.

We did not set out to find this. We stumbled onto it while looking for something else.

---

*These notes are kept for completeness. The conclusions drawn here should not be taken at face value.*
